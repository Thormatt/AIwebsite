Common Misconceptions

Let's separate AI reality from Silicon Valley storytelling

Myth: "AI will replace all jobs"

Reality: AI augments specific tasks, not entire roles. The jobs that disappear are rarely the ones people predict.

Myth: "AI understands context"

Reality: It's sophisticated pattern matching. LLMs don't "understand" —they predict statistically likely responses.

Myth: "More data equals better AI"

Reality: Quality beats quantity. Clean, relevant data from 100 examples often outperforms messy data from 10,000.

Myth: "AI is unbiased"

Reality: AI amplifies the biases in its training data. The question isn't if it's biased, but how and whether that matters for your use case.

Myth: "We need AI to stay competitive"

Reality: You need to solve real problems. Sometimes that's AI, often it's fixing your data infrastructure first.

Myth: "AI is plug-and-play"

Reality: Successful AI requires continuous iteration, monitoring, and adjustment. It's a capability, not a product.
