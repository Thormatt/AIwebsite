Common Misconceptions

Let's separate AI reality from Silicon Valley storytelling

Myth: "AI will replace all jobs"

Reality: AI augments specific tasks, not entire roles. The jobs that disappear are rarely the ones people predict.

Myth: "AI understands context"

Reality: It's sophisticated pattern matching. LLMs don't "understand" —they predict statistically likely responses.

Myth: "More data equals better AI"

Reality: Quality beats quantity. Clean, relevant data from 100 examples often outperforms messy data from 10,000.

Myth: "AI is unbiased"

Reality: AI amplifies the biases in its training data. The question isn't if it's biased, but how and whether that matters for your use case.

Myth: "We need AI to stay competitive"

Reality: You need to solve real problems. Sometimes that's AI, often it's fixing your data infrastructure first.

Myth: "AI is plug-and-play"

Reality: Successful AI requires continuous iteration, monitoring, and adjustment. It's a capability, not a product.

How is this different from hiring a consulting firm?

Consulting firms sell implementation teams and have incentive to expand scope. I provide vendor-neutral strategic counsel with no implementation arm to sell you. My only success metric is helping you make the right decisions for your organization—even if that means doing less, not more.

Do you build AI solutions or just advise?

I advise executives on AI strategy and can review technical architecture, but I don't take on client implementation projects. I build AI tools and systems for my own use (portfolio available by request), which keeps my advice unbiased and focused on your strategic interests, not my project revenue.

What's the time commitment for executives?

Discovery Assessment: 4-6 hours over 2 weeks (interviews, review sessions). Fractional engagement: 2-4 hours monthly for strategy sessions, plus async communication. I design engagements to maximize insight with minimal disruption to your schedule.

How quickly can we get started?

Discovery assessments typically start within 1-2 weeks of initial conversation. Fractional engagements can begin immediately if there's mutual fit. I limit active engagements to maintain quality and responsiveness.

What if our team already has AI expertise?

Even better. I work with your technical leads to pressure-test assumptions, identify blind spots, and provide strategic air cover for tough decisions. Think of me as an experienced advisor who's seen what works (and what doesn't) across dozens of implementations.

Do you sign NDAs and work under confidentiality?

Yes. All engagements include standard confidentiality protections. I never share client names, details, or learnings without explicit permission. Your competitive strategies and internal challenges remain completely confidential.
